Glomerulus
Li⁺ is freely filtered
No protein binding — nearly 100% of serum lithium enters the tubular filtrate here.
GFR matters. Dehydration, NSAIDs, ACEi, ARB, and aging all reduce GFR → less filtrate volume → less sodium delivered → proportionally more lithium reabsorbed throughout the tubule.
Proximal tubule — Issue 1
Serum lithium is a sodium story
NHE3 (apical) brings Na⁺ and Li⁺ from lumen into the cell. Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase (basolateral) pumps Na⁺ out — Li⁺ exits reasonably well here. High volume, proportional segment.
Sodium and lithium are reabsorbed here according to their proportion in the lumen. Less sodium means lithium makes up a larger fraction — so more lithium is absorbed.

Thick ascending limb
Same principle, smaller contribution
NHE3 and NKCC2 handle Na⁺ reabsorption here. Li⁺ reabsorbed alongside Na⁺ by the same proportional mechanism — ~3–10% of filtered load. No special asymmetry, no intracellular trapping.
Clinical significance here is serum level only — not structural injury. Less Na⁺ delivered means Li⁺ is a larger fraction. Same story as the proximal tubule, smaller volume.
Collecting duct — principal cells
Where lithium can cause structural damage
ENaC (apical) admits Li⁺ readily. NHE1 (basolateral) provides some exit but cannot keep pace. Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase does not move Li⁺. Net result: intracellular accumulation.
2a — Nephrogenic DI
2b — Direct toxicity
Result: AQP2 cannot insert → collecting duct impermeable to water despite normal ADH → nephrogenic DI.
  • Polydipsia
  • Polyuria
  • Decreased early morning urine osmolality — <300 consistent with NDI · 300–700 possible partial NDI
  • 24-hour fluid intake >2.5L — flag for NDI
Polyuria is not a nuisance side effect. It is evidence of intracellular accumulation. Can occur at any dose including therapeutic serum levels.

Amiloride protection
ENaC blocked — the collecting duct protected
Amiloride 5–10mg daily blocks ENaC channels, reducing Li⁺ entry. One channel remains partially open — entry is dramatically reduced, not eliminated.
Amiloride fixes: NDI resolves as AQP2 can now insert. Principal cell fibrosis risk drops. Polyuria-driven dehydration reduced — one serum level toxicity trigger indirectly addressed.
Dosing: 5mg twice daily. Monitor K⁺ and creatinine.

Issue 2b — direct toxicity
Intracellular accumulation at any dose — including therapeutic serum levels
Chronic intracellular Li⁺ accumulation → direct toxicity to principal cells → apoptosis → fibrotic replacement → irreversible CKD. Not a high-dose phenomenon. Duration of exposure matters as much as peak levels.
  • Polydipsia — signal of accumulation
  • Polyuria — signal of accumulation
  • Decreased early morning urine osmolality — signal of accumulation
  • Rising creatinine — late finding; structural damage already present
Amiloride fixes both 2a and 2b through the same mechanism — less ENaC entry, less intracellular accumulation, NDI resolves, fibrosis risk drops.
Fibrosis does not reverse when lithium is stopped. The window for prevention is while the functional signals are present.
