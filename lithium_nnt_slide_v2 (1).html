★ What these NNTs mean for primary care — confirmed talking points
NNT=4–5 across multiple indications. For comparison: aspirin for secondary MI prevention NNT≈50. Statins for secondary prevention NNT≈20–30. Lithium in the right patient is a high-yield intervention by any pharmacological standard.
Unipolar recurrence prevention NNT~3 is the most underused application in primary care. The patient with ≥3 depressive episodes on your panel — "recurrent MDD" on the problem list — is a guideline-supported candidate to discuss lithium. Tiihonen 2017 shows antidepressants alone HR 1.10 in this population.
Bipolar depression prevention: weakest RCT signal (NNT=14, borderline). But Ermis 2025 — 105,495 real patients — shows lithium is the only agent that reduced depression hospitalization. Every other treatment including antidepressants and antipsychotics increased it.
NNH/NNT=1.80 in augmentation (Vázquez 2021): only agent where the ratio exceeds 1.0. You must harm more patients than you help before canceling the benefit. SGAs=0.45, esketamine=0.71. This is the slide your audience will remember.
Lähteenvuo 2018 head-to-head: quetiapine HR 0.92 — only 7–8% reduction in rehospitalization. Lithium HR 0.67 — 33% reduction. Yet quetiapine is what primary care reaches for first. This is the prescribing gap in a single number.
All NNTs are vs placebo from RCT meta-analyses. Nordic registry HRs (Tiihonen, Lähteenvuo, Ermis) use within-individual Cox regression — each patient serves as their own control, eliminating between-person confounding. These are the strongest possible observational designs.
