Tremor
Characterizing the tremor
  • Fine postural tremor — nearly indistinguishable from essential tremor
  • Dose-dependent — higher serum levels correlate with more tremor
  • Check for tremor at baseline before starting — document what is pre-existing
Things that make it worse
  • Valproate co-prescription — common in this population
  • Lamotrigine — also a common co-prescription, can worsen tremor
  • Higher lithium levels — revisit dose if tremor is bothersome
  • Caffeine · anxiety · fatigue — worth asking about
Management
First
Reassure — fine tremor is common and not harmful. Lower dose if clinically able.
If mild
Propranolol 10mg BID — titrate every 2–7 days as tolerated until improved.
The important differential
If the patient is on an antipsychotic — consider drug-induced parkinsonism before attributing tremor to lithium. Character is different: resting tremor, rigidity, bradykinesia. Address the antipsychotic, not the lithium.
