Part II

Lithium Is Unique

You can call it a mood stabilizer, but there is nothing else that is a version of it.
Spoken: The gravity analogy. "Like gravity. Like sunlight. It falls through the levers and molecules of our biology in a certain way, creating multiple effects." Then patient quotes slide.
Ghaemi anchor: "There is no minimum dose." 50mg patient. Clinical observations: deeper sleep, no longer thinking about suicide. Forum voices: relief like painkiller, zero suicidal thoughts 9 months later, only medication that enabled real remission.

Lithium and the Brain — Beyond Mood

Alzheimer's Prevention
  • Aron & Yankner, Nature 2025 — lithium deficiency associated with earlier Alzheimer's onset
  • Kessing et al. 2008 — long-term lithium use associated with reduced dementia incidence
  • GSK-3β inhibition — one proposed mechanism for neuroprotective effect
Neuroprotection
  • Promotes BDNF expression — neuronal survival and plasticity
  • Reduces tau phosphorylation — relevant to Alzheimer's pathology
  • Signal seen at low doses; prescription dose not required for all effects
Sleep & Circadian
  • Clock gene regulation — lithium lengthens circadian period
  • Patients frequently report deeper, more restorative sleep early in treatment
  • Dose required for circadian effects not definitively established
What We Don't Know
  • Which effects require prescription dose vs. OTC doses vs. nutritional levels
  • Biomarker for lithium response does not exist — offer to all appropriate patients
  • Longitudinal registries (European nationalized systems) carry strongest signals
Aron & Yankner, Nature 2025 · Kessing et al. JAMA 2008 · Gildengers 2026 JAMA Neurology · Nunes microdose trial · Ghaemi Lithium Masterclass
Spoken: "Some people wonder if lithium is a micronutrient." The gravity analogy extended — taking gravity away from an adult weakens them; keep it from a child and they develop differently. We cannot know which patients will benefit from which effects. The doses required for dementia prevention, longevity, sleep and circadian regulation are not definitively known.

The Risks of Lithium

Big and Medium
Spoken intro: "I think the biggest one is the kidney — though there is room for discussion. Next is overall serum toxicity. Then pregnancy, then thyroid, then the things that people may quit for: diarrhea, tremor, acne, hair loss."
Interactive nephron hub. Click glomerulus, proximal tubule, ascending limb, collecting duct. Key: serum level toxicity (proximal tubule, sodium story) and collecting duct toxicity are SEPARATE mechanisms requiring SEPARATE conversations. Amiloride fixes 2a and 2b but does NOT protect against level toxicity from volume depletion.
(Some of) The Updated Math of Lithium in Pregnancy
The old narrative — and why it was wrong
Lithium causes Ebstein's anomaly. Based on a voluntary registry of cases only — healthy exposed pregnancies were not reported. Relative risk was dramatically overstated.
Boyle et al. 2017, Cardiology in the Young — European registry, 5+ million pregnancies, 250 cases of Ebstein's anomaly. None in lithium-exposed women. Association found with mental illness and psychotropic exposure broadly — not lithium specifically.
Confounding by indication. Comparing lithium-exposed women to the general population inflates apparent risk. When compared to women with similar psychiatric comorbidities not on lithium, elevated MCM risk is substantially attenuated — Fornaro et al.: OR 2.03 vs any unexposed group, 1.75 vs matched comparator group.
What lithium is NOT associated with
  • →Spontaneous abortion
  • →Preterm birth
  • →Low birth weight
  • →Developmental issues post in utero exposure (Poels et al. 2018)
Fornaro et al. 2020, Am J Psychiatry · Poels et al. 2018, Eur Child Adolesc Psychiatry
Untreated illness also carries risk
  • →Relapse, hospitalization, poor prenatal care
  • →Suicide risk in untreated bipolar disorder in pregnancy is not trivial
  • →In utero metabolic effects of untreated mental illness are difficult to separate from medication effects — the comparison group matters
  • →Poels et al. 2018: no developmental issues with lithium exposure — signal found with antipsychotics, not lithium
  • →Postpartum period is high relapse risk — continuation often strongly indicated

Clinical management in pregnancy
  • →Fetal echocardiogram at 16–20 weeks
  • →Monitor lithium levels frequently — volume of distribution increases in pregnancy, levels drop
  • →Target lithium level <0.64 mEq/L one week prior to delivery — reduces risk of neonatal hypotonia (Fornaro et al. 2020)
  • →Increase levothyroxine dose by 20% in pregnancy if hypothyroid (Dickens et al. 2019, JAMA)
  • →Neonatal monitoring at delivery — transient effects, manageable
  • →Shared decision-making with obstetrics and psychiatry
Breastfeeding
  • →Has been done — not automatically contraindicated, requires careful assessment
  • →Infant lithium levels may be higher in first 1–2 weeks, usually undetectable after that
  • →May want to supplement with formula early on
  • →First month infant growth can be slow — monitor weight
  • →Watch for outliers with higher infant lithium levels
  • →Consult ahead of time if possible — MC3
Heinonen et al. 2022, Acta Paediatrica — 30 mother-infant dyads
MCM = major congenital malformation · CVM = cardiovascular malformation
Boyle et al. 2017, Cardiology in the Young · Fornaro et al. 2020, Am J Psychiatry · Patorno et al. 2017, NEJM · Dickens et al. 2019, JAMA · Poels et al. 2018, Eur Child Adolesc Psychiatry · Heinonen et al. 2022, Acta Paediatrica
Pregnancy: Ebstein's risk overstated — Boyle 2017 5M pregnancies, 250 Ebstein's, none in lithium-exposed. Confounding by indication. Fornaro: OR 2.03 vs unexposed, 1.75 vs matched (200 covariates). Not associated with SA/preterm/low birth weight/developmental issues. Dose table: <300mg and 300-600mg comparable to peers, >900mg NNH 39. Breastfeeding: has been done, Heinonen 2022 30 dyads.
10% background, 18% on lithium. Target TSH <2, push toward 1 if mood symptoms persist. Do NOT apply general population threshold (5/7/10) to mood disorder patients. Subclinical hypothyroidism contributes to treatment resistance. Treatment: levothyroxine. Not a reason to stop.
Fine postural tremor. Check baseline — your only reference point. Valproate and lamotrigine worsen it. Management: reassure/lower dose → propranolol 10mg BID. Important differential: drug-induced parkinsonism (resting, pill-rolling) vs lithium/essential (postural, fine).
Skin: acne (mention before starting), hair thinning (minoxidil), psoriasis (ask history, refer derm, not automatic stop). Calcium: ~4% high estimate, lithium shifts setpoint, usually asymptomatic. Screen q6-12 months. If calcium AND PTH both elevated → refer endocrine. May require parathyroidectomy. Not reversible with dose reduction.
Part IV

The How
of Dosing

Spoken intro before formulations slide.
No minimum dose (Ghaemi). 150mg IR at bedtime. Once daily — timing matters only for checking a level. Observe: 2-month minimum window. Titrate toward 0.6-0.75 mEq/L if not responding. At 150-300mg: no level required. Clinical effect may lag 10 days after dose change including reductions. Elderly and frail: start lower, check earlier.
OTC lithium orotate. 5mg orotate ≈ 25mg carbonate. For cautious starters, migraineurs, patients who want to test the waters. "The biology does not require a prescription dose to begin."
Part V

Modern Day
Monitoring

Insulin and warfarin have sick day rules. So does lithium. Establish the plan at prescribing.
Spoken intro before monitoring slides.
TSH: baseline, 3 months, q6 months. Creatinine: baseline, q6-12 months. Calcium: q6-12 months. Levels: not required at 150-300mg. Check after 5 days minimum post dose change approaching 0.4+. Stable: q6 months. Polyuria / polydipsia: early collecting duct signal, NOT nuisance. EMUO <300 = collecting duct signal → amiloride consideration.
