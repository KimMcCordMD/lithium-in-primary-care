Thyroid Change is Common. Discuss Early, Treat Early.
Background and lithium risk
~10%
Background population rate of hypothyroidism
~18%
Average rate on lithium (Meyer & Stahl, Lithium Handbook)
Discuss before starting lithium. Plan to treat hypothyroidism if it develops — it is not a reason to stop.
  • Female sex, older age, and autoimmune history increase baseline risk further
Screening protocol
  • Baseline  TSH before starting
  • 3 months  First recheck
  • Every 6 months  Ongoing if stable
  • With any symptoms  Do not wait for the next scheduled check

Target TSH <2. Consider pushing toward TSH ~1 if mood symptoms are still present.
Do not apply the general population threshold. Allowing TSH to drift to 5, 7, or 10 is not advised in mood disorder patients on lithium. Subclinical hypothyroidism contributes to treatment resistance.
Treatment: levothyroxine.
Pregnancy
  • Renal clearance increases significantly during pregnancy — lithium levels may fall
  • Increase lithium dose by ~20% during pregnancy to maintain therapeutic levels
  • Monitor levels more frequently — monthly in second and third trimester
  • Reduce back to pre-pregnancy dose immediately postpartum — clearance drops rapidly
Note: lithium can cause thyroiditis. A suppressed TSH in a lithium patient warrants further evaluation.
